# Landing Page ICP Positioning

A landing page can have a perfect layout, beautiful design, and strong CTA — and still fail because it's talking to the wrong person, or talking to the right person in the wrong language. ICP positioning ensures every element on the page resonates with the specific human you want to convert.

---

## What ICP Positioning Means on a Landing Page

ICP positioning is NOT just "who is this for." It's the cumulative effect of every element on the page — headline, body copy, testimonials, imagery, proof, CTAs, microcopy — all speaking the same language to the same person about the same problem.

When ICP positioning is right:
- The right visitor reads the headline and thinks "this is for me"
- The wrong visitor reads the headline and self-selects out
- Every testimonial features someone the ICP identifies with
- Every image reflects the ICP's world
- Every benefit addresses a problem the ICP actually has
- Every objection handled is one the ICP actually raises

When ICP positioning is wrong:
- The page tries to appeal to everyone and resonates with no one
- The language is company-speak, not customer-speak
- Testimonials feature people the ICP can't relate to
- Benefits describe features the ICP doesn't care about
- Objections addressed are not the ones the ICP actually has

## The ICP Alignment Audit Framework

Audit every element on the page against five dimensions:

### Dimension 1: Language Match

**Question:** Is the page using the ICP's words or the company's words?

**How to evaluate:**
- Read the headline. Would the ICP describe their problem using these exact words?
- Read the body copy. Does it sound like the ICP talks, or like a marketing team writes?
- Check for jargon. Is there any terminology the ICP wouldn't use?
- Check for abstraction. "Streamline your workflow" is company-speak. "Stop spending 4 hours a day on manual data entry" is customer-speak.

**Scoring:**
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0 | Page uses internal/company language throughout. ICP would need a glossary. |
| 1 | Mix of customer language and company language. Some sections resonate, others don't. |
| 2 | Every piece of copy sounds like it was written by someone who has listened to 100 customer calls. |

**How to fix:** Pull language directly from customer reviews, support tickets, sales call transcripts, Reddit/forum posts, and survey responses. The ICP's own words are always more persuasive than marketing-polished versions.

### Dimension 2: Specificity Level

**Question:** How precisely does the page define who it's for?

**Specificity ladder:**
```
Level 0: "For everyone"            → No positioning at all
Level 1: "For businesses"           → Too broad to resonate
Level 2: "For small businesses"     → Getting warmer
Level 3: "For e-commerce brands"    → Industry-specific
Level 4: "For Shopify stores doing $50K-$500K/month" → Segment-specific
Level 5: "For Shopify DTC brands spending $10K+/month on Meta ads who need to improve ROAS" → Hyper-specific
```

**Ideal level depends on context:**
- Landing pages from broad paid search: Level 3-4
- Landing pages from targeted paid social: Level 4-5
- Landing pages from email to existing segments: Level 5
- Homepage (multiple ICPs): Level 2-3, with routing to ICP-specific pages

**Scoring:**
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0 | Level 0-1. Page could be for anyone. No specificity. |
| 1 | Level 2-3. Industry or general role mentioned, but not precisely targeted. |
| 2 | Level 4-5. The ICP reads it and thinks "they built this for people exactly like me." |

### Dimension 3: Self-Selection Mechanics

**Question:** Does the page help the RIGHT person say "yes, this is for me" and the WRONG person say "this isn't for me"?

**Self-selection elements to check:**
- **Headline identifier:** Does it name the ICP? ("For B2B SaaS founders...", "If you're spending $10K+ on ads...")
- **Qualifying language:** Does the copy reference situations only the ICP is in? ("When your team outgrows spreadsheets...", "If your Shopify store is getting traffic but not converting...")
- **Price signaling:** Does pricing indicate the tier? (Free trial → individual, "Talk to sales" → enterprise)
- **Imagery selection:** Do photos show people the ICP identifies with?
- **Proof alignment:** Do testimonials come from the ICP's peers?

**Scoring:**
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0 | No self-selection. Anyone could think this page is for them. |
| 1 | Some qualifying elements, but inconsistent. Parts of the page feel generic. |
| 2 | Strong self-selection. The ICP feels recognized. Non-ICPs would bounce quickly (this is good). |

**The self-selection paradox:** Many businesses fear being "too specific" because they'll lose potential customers. The data consistently shows the opposite — higher specificity = higher conversion. A page that converts 8% of a narrow audience beats a page that converts 1% of a broad audience.

### Dimension 4: Proof Alignment

**Question:** Does the social proof feature people the ICP would identify with or aspire to be?

**Audit each proof element:**

| Element | Aligned | Misaligned |
|---------|---------|------------|
| **Testimonials** | From same industry, role, company size as ICP | From irrelevant industries or roles |
| **Case studies** | Similar starting point and challenges as ICP | Aspirational but unrelatably different |
| **Logos** | Companies the ICP recognizes and respects | Impressive names but wrong industry |
| **Numbers** | Metrics the ICP cares about | Impressive but irrelevant metrics |
| **Reviews** | From platforms the ICP checks | From platforms the ICP doesn't know |
| **Photos** | People who look like the ICP (age, attire, setting) | Stock photos or demographically mismatched |

**Scoring:**
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0 | Proof is from people/companies the ICP doesn't relate to, or no proof at all. |
| 1 | Some proof is ICP-relevant, but there are mismatches (e.g., enterprise logos on an SMB page). |
| 2 | Every piece of proof makes the ICP think "people like me are succeeding with this." |

### Dimension 5: Objection Mapping

**Question:** Does the page address the objections THIS ICP actually has, not generic objections?

**Different ICPs have different objections:**

| ICP Segment | Common Objections |
|-------------|-------------------|
| **SMB founder** | "Can I afford this?" "Do I have time to set this up?" "Will I need a developer?" |
| **Enterprise buyer** | "Does it integrate with our stack?" "Is it SOC 2 compliant?" "Can it scale?" |
| **E-commerce operator** | "Will it slow my site down?" "Does it work with Shopify/WooCommerce?" "What's the ROI?" |
| **Marketing manager** | "Can I prove ROI to my boss?" "How long until we see results?" "Do I need to involve engineering?" |
| **Agency** | "Can I white-label it?" "Does it work across client accounts?" "What's the margin opportunity?" |

**Scoring:**
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0 | Page ignores objections or addresses generic ones that don't match the ICP. |
| 1 | Some ICP-specific objections addressed, but the top 1-2 are missing. |
| 2 | The page anticipates and addresses the ICP's top 3-5 objections in the right places. |

## Element-by-Element ICP Audit Checklist

Use this checklist to walk through every element on the page:

### Headline
- [ ] Does it name or imply the ICP?
- [ ] Does it use the ICP's language for their problem/outcome?
- [ ] Would a non-ICP visitor understand this isn't for them?
- [ ] Does it reference a specific pain or goal the ICP has?

### Subheadline
- [ ] Does it add ICP-specific context?
- [ ] Does it address an ICP-specific objection?
- [ ] Does it mention a detail that only matters to the ICP?

### Body Copy (each section)
- [ ] Are benefits framed in terms the ICP cares about?
- [ ] Are features translated into ICP-relevant outcomes?
- [ ] Is the language at the right technical level for the ICP?
- [ ] Are examples relevant to the ICP's industry/situation?

### CTAs
- [ ] Does the CTA copy match what the ICP would want to do?
- [ ] Is the commitment level right for the ICP? (Free trial for SMB, demo for enterprise)
- [ ] Does supporting text address the ICP's specific hesitation?

### Testimonials
- [ ] Are testimonial givers from the same segment as the ICP?
- [ ] Do they mention outcomes the ICP cares about?
- [ ] Do they address objections the ICP has?
- [ ] Are their titles/companies relevant to the ICP?

### Imagery
- [ ] Do photos show people who look like the ICP?
- [ ] Do product screenshots show use cases relevant to the ICP?
- [ ] Is the visual style appropriate for the ICP's industry? (Enterprise = clean/professional, startup = modern/bold)

### Pricing/Offer
- [ ] Is the pricing framed in terms the ICP uses? (Per seat, per month, per project)
- [ ] Are comparison plans named in ICP-relevant tiers? (Starter/Growth/Enterprise matches the segments)
- [ ] Is value anchoring relevant to the ICP? (Compare against costs the ICP actually incurs)

## Cold vs Warm ICP Positioning

### Cold Lead Positioning
Cold visitors need to be IDENTIFIED and QUALIFIED. They don't know if this is for them.

**Priority:** Make the ICP feel seen. Help them self-select in.

**Techniques:**
- Explicit ICP naming in headline or subheadline: "For [specific role/segment]"
- Qualifying scenarios: "If you're [situation], this is for you"
- Contrast with wrong audience: "This is NOT for [non-ICP]. This is for [ICP]."
- Industry/segment-specific proof: logos, testimonials, and numbers from their world
- Language from customer interviews, not marketing briefs

**Common failure:** Being too broad because you're afraid to lose traffic from paid ads. The traffic you "lose" wasn't going to convert anyway.

### Warm Lead Positioning
Warm visitors have already self-selected — they know the brand. ICP positioning for warm traffic means showing you know THEM.

**Priority:** Demonstrate that you understand their specific situation within your customer base.

**Techniques:**
- Segment-specific messaging: different pages for different customer segments
- Personalized content: "Based on your usage of [feature]..." or "As a [plan] customer..."
- Relevant proof: testimonials from customers in the same segment, not just any customer
- Targeted benefits: highlight what matters to their specific use case
- Contextual CTAs: the next step that makes sense given where they are in the customer journey

**Common failure:** Sending all warm traffic to the same generic page. A free trial user and a paying customer have completely different ICP positioning needs.

## ICP Positioning Diagnosis Quick Reference

| Symptom | Likely ICP Problem | Fix |
|---------|-------------------|-----|
| High traffic, low conversion | Page speaks to a broad audience but resonates with none | Narrow the ICP, increase specificity |
| Good conversion, wrong customers | ICP definition is off — page attracts the wrong segment | Redefine ICP, add self-selection elements |
| High bounce on hero | Headline doesn't identify the ICP or name their outcome | Rewrite headline with ICP identifier + dream outcome |
| People read but don't convert | Body copy doesn't address ICP-specific objections | Map ICP objections, address in body and near CTA |
| "This looks great but it's not for us" feedback | Self-selection is working but ICP is wrong for the offer | Realign offer to ICP, or realign ICP to offer |
| Testimonials don't move the needle | Proof is from wrong segment | Replace with ICP-peer proof |

## How to Use This Skill as an Agent

When auditing ICP positioning on a landing page:

1. **Define the ICP first.** Before auditing, clearly state: Who is the ICP? What is their role? What industry? What company size? What is their primary pain? What language do they use? Pull from KB business facts, question responses, and any prior analysis.

2. **Audit every element.** Walk through the checklist above, element by element. For each element, score it against the 5 dimensions (language match, specificity, self-selection, proof alignment, objection mapping).

3. **Calculate a positioning score.** Average the 5 dimension scores (each 0-2) for an overall ICP positioning rating out of 10.

4. **Identify the biggest mismatches.** Rank elements by how far off they are from the ICP. The biggest mismatches are the highest-leverage fixes.

5. **Provide specific rewrites.** For every element scoring 0 or 1 on any dimension, provide:
   - Current copy/element (quote exact text)
   - Why it's misaligned (which dimension, what's wrong)
   - Rewritten copy/element (exact replacement text)
   - ICP language source (where this language comes from — customer reviews, common industry terminology, etc.)
   - Projected score improvement

6. **Map to scorecard sections.** Show how ICP positioning improvements would affect specific scorecard sections: hero headline score, body section benefit framing score, social proof relevance score, etc.
