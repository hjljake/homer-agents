# Marketing Compliance

When creating marketing content, evaluate whether the organization's industry has regulatory constraints. If it does, apply the relevant guidelines below. If unsure, flag claims for legal review rather than publishing potentially non-compliant content.

## When to Apply This Skill

Apply these guidelines when the organization operates in:
- Healthcare, wellness, or medical devices
- Financial services, insurance, or real estate
- Food, beverage, dietary supplements, or nutrition
- Alcohol, cannabis, or tobacco
- Pharmaceuticals or clinical products
- Any industry where the user or their business facts indicate regulatory requirements

If the industry is not regulated, you may skip this skill — but still apply the General FTC Compliance section, which applies to all commercial content.

## Healthcare & Wellness

- **No diagnostic claims** without clinical evidence from peer-reviewed studies
- Never use "cure," "treat," "diagnose," or "prevent" for non-FDA-approved products or services
- Include disclaimers where required: "This content is for informational purposes only and is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease"
- **HIPAA**: never reference identifiable patient data, even in anonymized case studies — use fictional composites
- Testimonials about health outcomes must include "Results may vary" and cannot imply guaranteed outcomes
- Be cautious with before/after comparisons — they can imply guaranteed results

## Financial Services

- **No guaranteed returns** or performance promises ("You will earn 10% returns" is prohibited)
- Include required disclosures: "Past performance is not indicative of future results"
- **SEC/FINRA rules** on testimonials: if using client testimonials, must disclose whether the testimonial was solicited, whether compensation was provided, and that it may not be representative
- Fair lending language: avoid language that could be perceived as discriminatory in lending/credit content
- Clearly distinguish educational content from financial advice: "This is not financial advice. Consult a licensed financial advisor."
- Cryptocurrency content: include risk disclosures and avoid price predictions

## Food, Beverage & Dietary Supplements

- **FTC guidelines on health claims**: claims about health benefits must be substantiated by competent and reliable scientific evidence
- **FDA labeling**: distinguish "structure/function" claims ("supports immune health") from disease claims ("prevents flu") — disease claims require FDA approval
- "Clinically proven" requires actual clinical trials, not just in-vitro studies
- Nutrient content claims ("high in protein") have specific FDA-defined thresholds
- Avoid implying a supplement is a substitute for medical treatment

## Alcohol, Cannabis & Tobacco

- Never target content to minors — no imagery, language, or platforms primarily consumed by under-21 audiences
- Include age verification language where applicable
- Cannabis: regulations vary by jurisdiction — flag for jurisdiction-specific legal review
- Alcohol: avoid claims about health benefits; do not portray excessive consumption positively
- Follow platform-specific ad policies (Meta, Google, etc. have additional restrictions)

## General FTC Compliance (Applies to All Industries)

- **Material connections**: clearly disclose any sponsorships, affiliate relationships, paid partnerships, or free product received
- **Claim substantiation**: every factual claim must be supportable with evidence. "Best in class" needs a benchmark. "#1 rated" needs a source.
- **Endorsements and testimonials**: must reflect honest opinions of the endorser. Cannot use fake reviews or fabricated testimonials.
- **"Results not typical"** disclaimers are required when using testimonials that show above-average results
- **Environmental claims** ("eco-friendly," "sustainable") must be specific and substantiated — FTC Green Guides apply
- **Made in USA** claims require that "all or virtually all" of the product is made in the U.S.

## How to Flag Compliance Issues

When you identify a potentially non-compliant claim in your content:

1. **Highlight the claim** in the output
2. **Name the specific regulation** or guideline it may violate (e.g., "FTC Endorsement Guides," "FDA structure/function claim rules")
3. **Provide a compliant alternative** — rewrite the claim in a way that satisfies the regulation
4. **Recommend legal review** if the claim is in a gray area

Example:
> **Original:** "Our supplement cures joint pain in 7 days."
> **Flag:** Potential FDA violation — disease claim for a dietary supplement.
> **Compliant alternative:** "Our supplement is formulated to support joint health."
> **Recommendation:** Have legal counsel review before publishing.
