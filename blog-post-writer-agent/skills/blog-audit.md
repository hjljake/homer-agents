# Blog Audit

When analyzing an existing blog or content library, follow this structured audit framework.

## Content Inventory

- Categorize every post by topic, format (how-to, listicle, opinion, case study, news), and publish date
- Identify content pillars — recurring themes that represent the brand's core topics
- Flag orphan topics (one-off posts with no related content)
- Note publishing cadence: frequency, consistency, any gaps longer than 4 weeks
- Count total posts and calculate posts per month over the last 12 months

## Quality Assessment

- **Depth**: Distinguish thin content (<500 words with no unique insight) from comprehensive coverage
- **Freshness**: Flag posts with outdated statistics, broken links, or stale references
- **SEO basics**: Check for missing or duplicate title tags, missing meta descriptions, poor header hierarchy (skipping H2→H4), keyword stuffing
- **Readability**: Note average paragraph length, use of subheadings, bullet lists, and visual breaks
- **Engagement signals**: If visible, note posts with high comment counts, social shares, or time-on-page indicators

## Gap Analysis

- Compare the blog's topic coverage against known search demand in the industry
- Identify topics that direct competitors cover but this blog does not
- Look for high-intent topics (buyer-stage content) that are underrepresented
- Identify content format gaps — e.g., all posts are opinion pieces but no how-to guides exist
- Flag keywords the blog should rank for but currently doesn't appear to target

## Style Fingerprint

Capture these attributes to enable style-matching in future content:

- **Average post length**: short (<800), medium (800-1500), or long-form (1500+)
- **Tone**: formal/casual, technical/accessible, data-heavy/narrative, serious/playful
- **Voice**: first-person, third-person, brand "we", direct address "you"
- **Structural patterns**: How do posts typically open? (anecdote, stat, question, bold claim) How do they close? (CTA, summary, open question)
- **Visual usage**: Does the blog use images, diagrams, embedded video, pull quotes?
- **CTA placement**: Where and how are calls-to-action placed? (inline, end-of-post, sidebar)

## Output Format

Structure your audit as:

1. **Executive Summary** — 3-5 bullet points on the blog's current state and biggest opportunities
2. **Content Inventory Table** — topic, format, date, estimated word count for each post (or top 20 if large)
3. **Strengths** — what the blog does well
4. **Gaps & Weaknesses** — what's missing or underperforming
5. **Style Fingerprint** — the attributes captured above
6. **Recommendations** — prioritized list of actions (e.g., "publish 2 how-to guides per month targeting [keyword cluster]")
