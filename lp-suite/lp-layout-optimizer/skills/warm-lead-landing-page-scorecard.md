# Warm Lead Landing Page Scorecard

A grading system for evaluating and building landing pages that convert warm traffic — visitors arriving from email campaigns, retargeting ads, SMS, organic social, in-app notifications, or partner channels who **already have some relationship** with the brand.

---

## How to Use This Scorecard

Every landing page gets scored across 7 sections. Each section has specific criteria scored 0–2 (0 = missing/broken, 1 = present but weak, 2 = exceptional). Maximum score: 100 points.

**Point allocation is weighted for warm traffic psychology.** Warm visitors already know who you are — they don't need to be sold on your credibility. They need offer clarity, message match with the channel that brought them, and minimal friction. Offer clarity and below-the-fold content carry disproportionate weight because warm visitors are evaluating the specifics of THIS offer, not deciding if you're trustworthy.

**Before you score anything, answer these three questions:**

1. Who is the ICP hitting this page, and what is their existing relationship with the brand? (Subscriber, customer, trial user, social follower)
2. What is the single action you want them to take?
3. What message or channel brought them here, and what was promised?

If you can't answer all three, the page isn't ready to be scored. The channel-to-page journey must be defined first.

---

## The Value Equation (Score Every Element Against This)

Every component on the page either increases or decreases perceived value. If an element doesn't serve one of these four levers, it's noise. Cut it.

**Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)**

The numerator is what makes them want it. The denominator is what makes them hesitate.

**For warm leads specifically:** The perceived likelihood of achievement is already HIGHER than cold traffic — they've seen your brand, maybe used your product, and have some baseline trust. Your page should leverage this existing trust and focus on making the specific offer irresistible and the action effortless. The denominator (time/effort) is where warm leads are lost — they expect a frictionless, personalized experience from a brand they already know.

## SECTION 1: HERO SECTION (20 points)

*Warm visitors arrive with context. They clicked an email, an ad, or a notification — they already know something about you. The hero's job is not to introduce the brand but to deliver on the promise of the referring channel and get to the point fast.*

### 1A. Headline (8 points)

| Criteria | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|----------|---|---|---|
| **Message Match**: Does the headline deliver on the promise made in the email, ad, or notification that brought them here? If the email said "Your exclusive 30% off" and the landing page says "Welcome to Our Store," that's a disconnect. | No connection to referring channel | Loosely related but different language or framing | Direct continuation of the referring message — visitor feels "yes, this is what I clicked for" |
| **Relationship Acknowledgment**: Does the headline signal that this visitor is known, valued, or special? "Your exclusive offer," "As a [subscriber/customer/member]," "Because you [action they took]." | Treats the visitor like a stranger | Generic but not actively alienating | Visitor feels recognized and valued as someone with an existing relationship |
| **Offer Clarity**: Does the headline state what they're getting, clearly and immediately? Warm visitors don't need the "what we do" preamble — they need to know what THIS specific page is offering them. | Vague or generic — unclear what the offer is | Offer is mentioned but not front-and-center | The offer IS the headline. Specific, clear, immediate. |
| **Speed to Point**: Does the hero skip the brand introduction and get directly to the offer? Warm visitors already know you — every second spent re-explaining who you are is a second they're deciding to leave. | Full brand preamble before getting to the offer | Some unnecessary context before the point | Gets to the point immediately — no wasted words |

**Headline Score: ___ / 8**

### 1B. Subheadline (4 points)

| Criteria | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|----------|---|---|---|
| **Value Reinforcement**: Does it deepen the offer with specifics? What's included, what's different, what's the advantage of acting now? Warm visitors want details, not more persuasion. | Doesn't add information beyond the headline | Some additional detail but not compelling | Adds specific, valuable detail that makes the offer more attractive |
| **Exclusivity or Relevance Framing**: Does it reinforce why this offer is specifically for THEM? "Only for [segment]," "Based on your [activity]," "Available to [relationship type] only." | No personalization or segmentation signal | Mild relevance but not specific to their segment | Clear signal that this offer is tailored to their specific relationship with the brand |

**Subheadline Score: ___ / 4**

### 1C. CTA Button (4 points)

| Criteria | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|----------|---|---|---|
| **Frictionless Copy**: Does the CTA minimize perceived effort? Warm visitors should see low-friction language: "Claim Your Offer," "Activate My Discount," "Upgrade Now," "Yes, Send It." Higher-commitment language is OK for warm leads (vs cold) but it should still feel easy. | Generic ("Submit", "Click Here") | Somewhat specific but doesn't convey ease | Clear value, low friction, specific to the offer |
| **Visual Prominence**: Is the CTA the most prominent element, even more so than the headline? Warm visitors already have intent — the CTA just needs to be obvious and immediate. | Hidden or blends in | Visible but not dominant | Unmissable, highest contrast, impossible to miss |

**CTA Score: ___ / 4**

### 1D. Supporting Context in Hero (4 points)

| Criteria | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|----------|---|---|---|
| **Relevant Social Proof**: Is there proof that supports THIS specific offer (not just generic brand trust)? "12,000 subscribers upgraded last month," "Rated 4.9 by existing customers," results from people who took this specific action. | No supporting proof | Generic brand proof, not offer-specific | Proof that directly supports the specific action being asked |
| **Urgency/Availability Signal**: Is there a genuine reason to act now? Warm leads respond to real deadlines, limited availability, or exclusive windows. "Your offer expires [date]," "Only [X] spots remaining in your cohort." | No urgency, or fake urgency | Mild urgency without specifics | Genuine, specific, time-bound reason to act now |

**Supporting Context Score: ___ / 4**

## SECTION 2: BELOW-THE-FOLD SEQUENCE (20 points)

*Warm visitors scroll MORE than cold visitors because they already have interest. Below-the-fold content is where warm visitors make their final decision. This section should provide the specific details, comparisons, and reassurance they need to act.*

### 2A. Offer Details Section (8 points)

| Criteria | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|----------|---|---|---|
| **Specifics**: Does the page spell out exactly what the warm visitor gets? Feature lists, what's included, what's excluded, comparison to their current plan/product. Warm visitors are evaluating a specific decision, not browsing. | Vague about what's included | Some details but gaps in specifics | Complete, clear breakdown of exactly what they get |
| **Comparison/Upgrade Path**: If this is an upgrade, upsell, or plan change — is there a clear comparison showing what they have now vs. what they'll get? Warm visitors need to see the delta. | No comparison to current state | Some comparison but not comprehensive | Clear side-by-side: what they have now vs. what they'll get, with benefits highlighted |
| **Value Anchoring**: Is the price or ask anchored against something that makes it feel like a great deal? "Normally $X, your price: $Y." "This single feature paid for itself in 2 weeks for [customer]." | No value context for the ask | Some value framing but not compelling | Strong value anchor that makes the offer feel like a no-brainer |
| **FAQ / Objection Handling**: Does the page address the specific objections warm leads have? These are DIFFERENT from cold objections. Warm leads ask: "Is this worth it given what I already have?" "Can I go back if I don't like it?" "Will this disrupt my current setup?" | No objection handling | Some objections addressed | Top warm-lead objections anticipated and resolved |

**Offer Details Score: ___ / 8**

### 2B. Reinforcement Sections (8 points)

| Criteria | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|----------|---|---|---|
| **Results from Similar Users**: Are there case studies or testimonials from people who were in the same position (existing subscriber/customer who took this next step)? "I was on the free plan for 6 months before upgrading — wish I'd done it sooner." | No results from similar users | Generic testimonials, not from similar users | Specific results from people who made the exact same decision being asked |
| **Use-Case Specificity**: Does the page show how this offer applies to the visitor's specific situation? Not generic benefits but specific scenarios relevant to their segment. | Generic benefits only | Some relevant scenarios | Specific use cases that match the visitor's segment and needs |
| **Storytelling / Narrative Flow**: Does the page flow like a natural progression from interest to decision? Each section should answer the next logical question a warm visitor would ask. | Sections feel random or disconnected | Some logical flow | Each section answers the question the previous one raised |
| **Scannability**: Can someone skim headlines and bolded text and get the full picture? Warm visitors are more willing to read than cold visitors, but they still scan first. | Requires full read | Partially scannable | Headlines alone tell the complete story |

**Reinforcement Score: ___ / 8**

### 2C. Repeated CTAs (4 points)

| Criteria | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|----------|---|---|---|
| **Frequency**: Is the CTA repeated at natural decision points? After each major section that adds value or removes a doubt. | CTA only in hero and/or footer | 2 CTA placements | CTA appears at every natural decision point |
| **Progressive Copy**: Do repeated CTAs evolve in copy to match the reader's increasing conviction? Hero: "See What's Included" → Mid-page: "Start Your Upgrade" → Bottom: "Claim Your [Offer] Now." | Same generic copy everywhere | Mostly consistent | Copy progressively matches the reader's journey from interest to decision |

**Repeated CTAs Score: ___ / 4**

## SECTION 3: SOCIAL PROOF DEPTH (10 points)

*Warm visitors already have baseline trust. They don't need to be convinced your brand is legitimate — they need to be convinced that THIS specific action is worth taking. Proof should be targeted, not voluminous.*

| Criteria | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|----------|---|---|---|
| **Offer-Specific Proof**: Do testimonials and results reference the specific offer, product, or action being promoted — not just the brand in general? "The upgrade was worth every penny" beats "Great company!" | No offer-specific proof | Generic brand testimonials only | Proof directly validates the specific decision being asked |
| **Peer Proof**: Are there results from people the warm visitor would identify with? Same plan level, same industry, same company size, same use case. "As a fellow [segment]..." | No peer proof | Some relevant proof but not closely matched | Proof from peers the visitor would directly identify with |
| **Community/Momentum Signal**: Is there a signal that others in their cohort are taking this action? "Join 3,400 subscribers who upgraded this quarter," "Most popular choice among [segment]." Social pressure from peers they know. | No community signal | Weak signal ("many others") | Specific, credible momentum signal from their peer group |
| **Results Over Time**: Is there proof that shows outcomes OVER TIME, not just initial results? Warm leads want to know: does this keep delivering? "After 6 months, customers see an average of..." | No longitudinal proof | Initial results only | Clear evidence of sustained or growing results over time |
| **User-Generated Content**: Are there real reviews, social posts, or user screenshots — not just polished testimonials? UGC feels more authentic to warm leads who are already familiar with marketing polish. | No UGC or real user content | Some UGC but limited | Authentic user-generated content that feels real and relatable |

**TOTAL SOCIAL PROOF SCORE: ___ / 10**

## SECTION 4: OFFER CLARITY (20 points)

*This is the highest-weighted section for warm leads. They're already interested — now they need to understand exactly what they're getting, what it costs, and why it's worth it. Ambiguity kills warm-lead conversion faster than anything else.*

| Criteria | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|----------|---|---|---|
| **Explicit Deliverables**: Is every deliverable listed clearly? Not "premium features" but exactly which features, how many, what limits. Warm visitors have higher expectations for specificity because they already know the product. | Unclear what you actually get | Somewhat clear but gaps in specifics | Complete, granular list of exactly what's included and what's not |
| **Pricing Transparency**: Is pricing clear, confident, and fully disclosed? No surprise fees, no "contact us for pricing" when a price exists. Warm leads who feel tricked by hidden costs become detractors, not just lost leads. | Price hidden, confusing, or surprise fees at checkout | Price shown but lacking full cost breakdown | Fully transparent pricing with clear breakdown, no surprises |
| **Risk Reversal**: Is there a guarantee, trial period, downgrade path, or refund policy? For warm leads, the key risk is "what if I don't like the change?" They need to know they can go back. | No risk reversal | Weak or buried guarantee | Prominent risk reversal that addresses the specific concern of warm leads (e.g., "Downgrade anytime, no questions asked") |
| **Personalization Signals**: Does the page feel personalized to the visitor? Their name, their usage data, their segment, their history. Warm leads expect brands they know to know them back. | No personalization — feels like a generic page | Light personalization (segment-level) | Meaningful personalization based on their specific relationship and data |
| **Comparison to Current State**: Does the page clearly show what changes vs. what stays the same? Warm leads fear disruption to their current setup. "Everything you have now, PLUS..." | No comparison to their current experience | Some mention of continuity | Clear reassurance about what stays the same + clear display of what's new |
| **Exclusivity / Segment Framing**: Is the offer framed as exclusive to their segment? "Your subscriber-only price," "As a [plan] customer, you get..." Not a generic landing page that anyone could see. | Generic page — no segment framing | Some exclusivity language but not convincing | Clear, genuine exclusivity that makes the visitor feel like this is truly for them |
| **Terms Clarity**: Are the terms obvious? Billing frequency, contract length, cancellation policy, data migration — whatever applies. Warm leads who have to hunt for terms assume the worst. | Terms hidden or confusing | Terms exist but require searching | Terms clearly stated near the CTA, easy to find, no fine-print surprises |
| **Single Clear Path**: Is there one obvious action to take? Warm leads are more focused than cold leads, but competing CTAs still cause confusion. | Multiple competing paths | Mostly focused with minor distractions | One clear action, zero ambiguity |
| **Action Continuity**: Does clicking the CTA lead to a smooth, expected experience? No surprise redirects, no long forms, no "wait we need more info." If they're logged in, pre-fill their data. | Clicking CTA leads to an unexpected or friction-heavy experience | Reasonable post-click flow but could be smoother | Seamless: click → minimal steps → done. Pre-filled where possible. |
| **Value Recap Near CTA**: Is there a concise summary of what they get right next to the final CTA? Warm leads want one last confirmation before clicking. "You're getting: [list]. Your price: [price]. Guaranteed for [period]." | No value summary near CTA | Some value mention but not comprehensive | Clear, concise value recap immediately adjacent to the CTA |

**TOTAL OFFER CLARITY SCORE: ___ / 20**

## SECTION 5: MOBILE EXPERIENCE (10 points)

*Warm traffic from email and SMS is overwhelmingly mobile. If the page doesn't work perfectly on a phone, the email campaign — no matter how good — is wasted.*

| Criteria | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|----------|---|---|---|
| **Load Speed**: Does the page load in under 3 seconds on mobile? Warm visitors from email/SMS have slightly more patience than cold, but not much. Personalization scripts and dynamic content can slow pages — test with these enabled. | Over 5 seconds | 3–5 seconds | Under 3 seconds, even with personalization scripts active |
| **Hero on Mobile**: Is the full hero (headline, offer, CTA) visible on a phone screen without scrolling? This is especially critical for email traffic where the visitor's thumb is already near "back." | Hero requires scrolling to see CTA | Most of hero visible but CTA is cut off | Complete hero visible on first mobile screen |
| **Tap Targets**: Are buttons large enough to tap with a thumb? Minimum 48px height, ideally 56px+. Generous spacing between elements. | Small buttons, crowded tap targets | Mostly tappable but some tight spacing | All buttons are thumb-friendly with generous spacing |
| **Readability**: Is text large enough to read without zooming? 16px minimum body text. Works in both portrait and landscape. | Text too small, lines too long | Readable but not optimized | Perfect readability, comfortable line length, clear hierarchy |
| **Email Client Rendering**: Does the page display correctly when opened from in-app email browsers (Gmail app, Outlook mobile, Apple Mail)? These have different rendering engines than standard mobile browsers. | Page breaks in email client browsers | Minor rendering differences | Consistent experience across all major email client in-app browsers |

## SECTION 6: BRAND IDENTITY & DESIGN (10 points)

*Warm visitors already have a mental model of your brand. The landing page must match that model. Any disconnect between how they know you (from emails, product, social) and how this page looks creates cognitive friction.*

| Criteria | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|----------|---|---|---|
| **Brand Consistency**: Does the page feel like it belongs to the brand they already know? Colors, fonts, tone, imagery should match the emails, product, and social presence they're used to. | Looks like a different brand | Some brand elements but inconsistent with their experience | Unmistakably the same brand they know and already have a relationship with |
| **Channel Consistency**: Does the page's visual style match the channel that sent them? If the email was polished and professional, the landing page should match. Jarring style changes create distrust. | Significant disconnect from referring channel | Similar but noticeable differences | Seamless visual and tonal continuation from the referring channel |
| **Photography/Imagery**: Are images consistent with what the visitor has seen in emails, product, or social? Familiar imagery creates continuity; unfamiliar stock photos create disconnect. | Generic stock photos that don't match the brand experience | Some familiar imagery mixed with unfamiliar | Imagery feels consistent with and natural extension of the brand experience they know |
| **White Space & Breathing Room**: Does the page feel spacious and confident or cramped and desperate? Brands that know their audience don't need to cram every argument onto one page. | Cluttered, cramped, overwhelming | Adequate spacing but could breathe more | Spacious, intentional, confident |
| **Typography & Hierarchy**: Is the type hierarchy clear and consistent with the brand's other touchpoints? | Poor type choices or inconsistent with brand | Decent but not fully aligned with brand typography | Typography is consistent with the brand system and guides the eye effectively |

**TOTAL BRAND/DESIGN SCORE: ___ / 10**

## SECTION 7: TECHNICAL & TRUST FUNDAMENTALS (10 points)

*For warm traffic, technical issues are more damaging than for cold traffic because the visitor has expectations. They've interacted with your brand before — they expect things to work. Technical failures erode existing trust.*

| Criteria | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|----------|---|---|---|
| **SSL/Security**: Does the page have HTTPS and relevant trust badges? Warm visitors paying for an upgrade or entering payment info expect security indicators. | No HTTPS or trust indicators | HTTPS but no trust badges near payment/forms | HTTPS + trust badges + security indicators where payment or data is involved |
| **No Broken Elements**: Do all images load? Do all buttons work? Do all forms submit? Do personalization tokens render correctly? | Broken elements or raw merge tags visible (e.g., "Hi {{first_name}}") | Minor issues | Everything works perfectly, including dynamic/personalized content |
| **UTM & Tracking Integrity**: Do UTM parameters, tracking pixels, and attribution work correctly? Does the page break if UTM params are present in the URL? Can you accurately measure which email/channel drove conversions? | Tracking broken or page breaks with UTM params | Tracking partially works | Full tracking integrity — attribution works, page renders correctly with any URL parameters |
| **Session Continuity**: If the visitor is logged in, does the page recognize them? Can they complete the action without re-entering information the brand already has? Pre-filled forms, recognized login status, saved payment methods. | No session recognition — treats logged-in users as strangers | Partial recognition (e.g., knows they're logged in but doesn't pre-fill) | Full session continuity — recognizes user, pre-fills data, removes redundant steps |
| **Cross-Device Consistency**: Does the page work if the visitor opens the email on mobile but clicks through on desktop (or vice versa)? Links should work regardless of device, and the experience should be comparable. | Page breaks or looks significantly different across devices | Minor differences across devices | Consistent, polished experience on all devices the visitor might use |

### Grading Scale

**90–100: World-Class.** This page is perfectly optimized for warm traffic. The offer is clear, the experience is seamless, and the page feels like a natural extension of the relationship. A/B test small variations to squeeze out remaining gains.

**75–89: Strong.** The fundamentals are solid but 1–3 areas are creating unnecessary friction or missed opportunities. Most commonly: offer clarity could be sharper, personalization is missing, or the post-click experience has too much friction.

**60–74: Decent Foundation, Significant Gaps.** The page works but it's not leveraging the existing relationship. Common issues: treating warm visitors like cold traffic (over-explaining the brand), weak offer specifics, no personalization, or message mismatch with the referring channel.

**40–59: Needs Major Work.** The page is actively damaging the brand relationship. Warm visitors who click through with intent and find a confusing, generic, or broken experience don't just not convert — they lose trust. Fix the top 3 issues immediately.

**Below 40: Start Over.** The page is so disconnected from the warm visitor's expectations that it's doing more harm than good. Sending warm traffic here is worse than sending them nowhere. Rebuild with the channel-to-page journey as the foundation.

## WARM LEAD ANTI-PATTERNS

These mistakes are especially lethal for warm traffic:

1. **Repeating the full brand story.** They already know you. Skip the "Founded in 2019, we believe..." intro and get to the offer.
2. **Message mismatch.** The email says "Your exclusive 30% off" but the landing page says "Check out our pricing." The disconnect kills conversions.
3. **Generic messaging.** Treating warm visitors like strangers. No acknowledgment of their subscriber status, purchase history, or engagement level.
4. **Offering what they already have.** Promoting a feature they already use, or a product they already own. This signals you don't know your own customers.
5. **Over-qualifying with long forms.** You already have their name, email, and account data. Don't ask for it again. Pre-fill, auto-detect, or skip.
6. **Broken personalization.** "Hi {{first_name}}" or "Dear Valued Customer" is worse than no personalization at all. Test every token.
7. **No downgrade/undo path.** Warm leads fear commitment more than cost. "What if I don't like the upgrade?" needs a clear answer.
8. **Redirecting to a generic page.** Sending email subscribers to the same page paid search visitors land on. Warm leads deserve a dedicated experience.
9. **Ignoring the referring channel's promise.** Every click is a micro-contract. The email made a promise. The landing page must deliver on it.
10. **Making them log in again.** Warm leads who are already logged in should never see a login screen when they click through from email or in-app notification. Session persistence is expected.

## HOW TO USE THIS AS AN AGENT SKILL

When scoring a landing page as an AI agent:

1. **Map the full journey first.** Before scoring the page, understand: what channel sent them (email, SMS, retargeting, in-app), what was the message, and what was promised? The page score is meaningless without this context.

2. **Score message match before anything else.** If the referring message says X and the page says Y, flag this immediately. No amount of page optimization compensates for a message mismatch.

3. **Score offer clarity first and separately.** If Section 4 scores below 14/20, flag it as the priority fix. For warm leads, offer clarity is the single biggest conversion lever.

4. **Be specific in your scoring notes.** Don't just say "offer is unclear." Say "the page mentions '50% off' but doesn't specify: 50% off what? For how long? What's the normal price? What's included? Rewrite the offer section to include: [specific suggestion]."

5. **Provide a rewrite for any element scoring 0 or 1.** The scorecard is diagnostic, but the value is in the prescription.

6. **Evaluate the channel-to-page journey.** Score the landing page in the context of the referring channel. Read the email or ad that sent them. Does the page continue the conversation or start a new one?

7. **Check for personalization opportunities.** If the brand has the visitor's name, usage data, or segment info, flag every place personalization could improve the experience.

8. **Prioritize fixes by section weight.** Hero (20 pts) = Below-fold (20 pts) = Offer Clarity (20 pts) > Mobile + Brand + Technical (10 each) > Social Proof (10 pts). Fix the highest-weighted low scores first.

9. **Test the post-click experience.** Click the CTA and evaluate what happens next. For warm leads, the post-click experience (forms, checkout, confirmation) is as important as the page itself.

## QUICK-REFERENCE: THE 11/10 WARM-TRAFFIC HERO

For when you need to nail the hero for warm visitors:

**Headline**: Delivers on the referring channel's promise. Acknowledges the relationship. States the offer clearly and immediately. No brand intro — they already know you. Gets to the point in under 2 seconds.

**Subheadline**: Adds specific detail that makes the offer irresistible. Frames exclusivity: "Only for [their segment]." Answers "what do I get?" with precision.

**CTA**: Low-friction, high-clarity. Says what they get and how easy it is. "Claim My Offer" or "Activate My Upgrade" — not "Learn More." If they're logged in, the CTA might even be one-click.

**Supporting Proof**: Not generic brand trust — specific proof that this action is worth taking. "3,400 subscribers upgraded this month." Results from people who made this exact decision.

**Visual/Brand**: Immediately recognizable as the brand they know. Consistent with their email, product, and social experience. No visual disconnect. The page feels like a natural next step, not a detour.

**The test**: Send the referring message (email, ad, notification) to someone who already uses your product. After they click through, ask: "Does this page deliver on what the message promised? Do you feel recognized? Is it clear what to do?" If yes to all three, you're close to an 11.
